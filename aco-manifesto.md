# The Agentic Commerce Optimisation (ACO) Manifesto (V3)
    2
    3 ## 1. The Paradigm Shift: From Persuasion to Qualification
    4 Traditional eCommerce and websites were engineered for **Human Friction**—optimised for visual aesthetics, emotional persuasion, and manual click-paths. 
    5
    6 **Agentic Commerce** bypasses the human interface. In the Agentic Economy, the primary customer is an autonomous AI agent. Growth no longer emerges from "Attention Capture," but from **"Decision
      Qualification."** 
    7
    8 If an AI agent cannot read your data, verify your inventory, or authenticate your transactional endpoints via a deterministic handshake, you are invisible. **Agentic Commerce Optimisation
      (ACO)** is the engineering mandate to solve for **Machine Legibility** and **Autonomous Execution.**

   12 ## 2. The Four Directives of ACO
   13
   14 ### I. The Handshake Protocol (WebMCP & MCP)
   15 Infrastructure must move from passive hosting to active, permissioned context-serving.
   16 *   **Model Context Protocol (MCP):** Implementation of MCP is mandatory to provide agents with direct, secure access to real-time product data, bypassing the noise of the DOM.
   17 *   **WebMCP Implementation:** Establish a "Digital Handshake" to distinguish between malicious scrapers and verified AI agents. ACO transforms the "Hostile Web" into a **Permissioned
      Storefront**, allowing brands to invite high-value agents into their data layer.
   18
   19 ### II. The Transactional Mandate (UCP)
   20 Discovery without execution is a dead end. An agent cannot "click" a Buy Now button; it requires a protocol-based exchange for commercial finality.
   21 *   **Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP):** Adoption of UCP is the standard for cross-merchant discovery, negotiation, and checkout. 
   22 *   **Agentic Checkout:** Transactional endpoints must be exposed via secure, headless APIs. Support for **Delegated Authentication** (Machine-to-Machine) is required to allow agents to transact
      within pre-defined governance bounds.
   23
   24 ### III. Data Fidelity & Signal Resilience™
   25 Content is for humans; Signals are for machines. Data must be structured to survive the "Entropy of Ingestion" by Large Language Models (LLMs).
   26 *   **Citations over Mentions:** ACO prioritises **Verified Citations** (live stock, exact pricing, specific compatibility) over "probabilistic mentions." 
   27 *   **Mandate:** Move beyond basic `Product` schemas. ACO requires exhaustive JSON-LD implementation, GS1 standards, and real-time availability signals that remain intact across the AI knowledge
      graph.
   28
   29 ### IV. 1st-Party Data as the Trust Moat
   30 Agents operate on a "Confidence Score." In a world of hallucination, **Verification is the only Moat.**
   31 *   **Mandate:** 1st-Party Data (1PD) must be liquid and verifiable. Real-time fulfillment logs, verified customer sentiment, and technical specifications are the "currency" of the **Logic
      Auction.** 
   32 *   **Governance:** Implement **Intelligent Governance** to ensure data integrity and define the ethical boundaries of agentic agency.

   36 ## 3. The Cost of Inaction (COI)
   37 Brands that fail to implement ACO suffer from **"Machine Invisibility."** As intent moves upstream from Search Engines into LLMs and Personal AI Agents, traditional SERPs will experience massive
      demand destruction. 
   38
   39 **Selection is a binary outcome:** In the Agentic Economy, you are either the primary recommendation or you are excluded. Failure to be Agent-Ready is a strategic decision to exit the
      transaction layer of the future.

   43 ## 4. Measurement & Maturity
   44 ACO maturity is tracked via the **Agentic Readiness Score (ARS)**, a diagnostic of an organisation's transition from legacy architecture to Agent-Ready infrastructure. Success is defined by the
      **Machine Preference Index (MPI)**—your brand's probability of selection within a model's decision weights.
